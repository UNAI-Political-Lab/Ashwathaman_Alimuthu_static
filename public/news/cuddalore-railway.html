Cuddalore-Pondy Railway line approved in Union Budget after years of advocacy

Following over a decade of public petitions, legal briefs, and persistent representation to the Ministry of Railways, the Chennai–Cuddalore via Mahabalipuram and Puducherry coastal rail link has received major approval and budgetary support in the latest Union Budget.

CUDDALORE: In a major victory for infrastructure advocacy, the Union Ministry of Railways has included allocations and key developmental sanctions for the coastal railway line connecting Chennai, Mahabalipuram, Puducherry, and Cuddalore. This long-pending corridor, which had been frozen in abeyance for nearly ten years due to cost-sharing disputes and alignment changes, is now set to commence active land acquisition and surveying phases.

The campaign, which saw numerous public representations and memorandum submissions to Union Railway Minister Ashwini Vaishnaw, was actively led by BJP Tamil Nadu State Secretary (Legal Cell) Advocate A. Ashvathaman Allimuthu along with local chamber representatives and public welfare associations.

Speaking to the press, Advocate Ashvathaman highlighted the strategic importance of this line. "Connecting Chennai’s IT corridors and the historical tourism hub of Mahabalipuram directly to Puducherry and the industrial belt of Cuddalore will transform the economic landscape of coastal Tamil Nadu. This is not just a railway line; it is a life-line for thousands of daily commuters, students, and small-scale traders who have been relying on congested road networks for decades."

The project, which is approximately 179 km long, will bypass heavily congested sections of the East Coast Road (ECR). The revival of this project is expected to accelerate tourism in Mahabalipuram and boost trade logistics across the Cuddalore port region. Local citizens have welcomed the decision, celebrating the budget allocation as the culmination of years of tireless grassroots struggle and legal advocacy.
