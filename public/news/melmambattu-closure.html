Melmambattu chemical/liquor plant shut down following farmer protests

The Tamil Nadu Pollution Control Board (TNPCB) has ordered the immediate suspension of operations and closure of a chemical manufacturing unit in Melmambattu, Cuddalore, following weeks of intensive demonstrations by local farmers and a strong legal campaign.

CUDDALORE: Local residents and agricultural workers in Melmambattu Taluk are celebrating a landmark decision as the state pollution watch-dog issued closure orders to a major chemical and manufacturing facility operating in the region. The unit has been accused of violating environmental standards and discharging untreated chemical effluents into the surrounding lands, causing severe contamination of the local water table.

The legal campaign and public representation before the district collectorate and the TNPCB were spearheaded by Advocate A. Ashvathaman Allimuthu, who provided free legal counsel to the affected farming community. The protests intensified after laboratory tests of local tube-wells revealed high levels of hazardous chemical residues, rendering groundwater unfit for irrigation or domestic consumption.

"Agriculture is the backbone of Melmambattu. Allowing industrial units to pollute our water resources for short-term profits is a direct violation of the right to life and livelihood under Article 21," said Advocate Ashvathaman. "The closure order is a strong warning to polluters that the health of our citizens and our soil cannot be compromised."

The TNPCB has directed the local electricity board to disconnect power supply to the facility and has mandated that the owners undertake immediate soil and water remediation measures. Local farmer collectives have expressed their deep gratitude to the legal team for standing by them and securing their agricultural future.
